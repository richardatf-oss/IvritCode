What is IvritCode?

IvritCode treats the Hebrew alphabet as a tiny instruction set. Each letter is a command. The dots and melodies of the text become modifiers. Together they form a programmable, verifiable way to describe processes in the language of the letters themselves.

On the surface, it can be played with like a toy‚Äîchange some numbers, watch the letters work. Under the hood, its execution trace is designed to live comfortably inside modern tools like finite-field virtual machines and zero-knowledge proofs.
